With hundreds of coffee shops to choose from, you don't have to walk very far to grab a cup of coffee in San Francisco. But when it comes to getting the coffee you love - like, Starbucks, Peet's and Philz - how far do you actually have to go?

Using Yelp data to locate the geographical positioning of San Francisco's most abundant coffee shop chains, Hoodline crunched the numbers to determine just how close these outposts exist in relation to one another - and how San Francisco compares to other cities.

Not surprisingly, San Francisco's most prolific coffee shop chain is Starbucks, which is the country's largest coffee chain, with 12078 stores nationwide. There are 97 Starbucks locations in San Francisco, and according to the most recent census projections, that means there's roughly one Starbucks per 8978 residents (not including daily commuters and tourists).

Although the average distance between San Francisco's Starbuck's is just under a quarter of a mile (1414 feet), the shortest distance between two locations is 71 feet. That's how far it is between the Starbuck's at 280 King St and the Starbuck's at 298 King St.

The second most abundant coffee shop chain in San Francisco is Peet's, with 44 locations citywide.

Compared to Starbuck's, when it comes to Peet's, the average distance between stores is 3048 feet.
